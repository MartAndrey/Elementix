    Basic scheme of weaknesses and strengths

    In the image just below you can see a simple summary of the bonuses or damage reductions. Each column shows how much damage the item in the top row (yellow row) does to the rest of the items. Any type not listed in that column means it deals 100% damage, with no bonus or reduction.

    This is the meaning of the multipliers on each row:

    • X2: The item on the top row deals double damage to the items indicated on the green (second) row.
    • X1.5: The element on the top row one-fifth normal damage to the indicated elements on the orange (third) row.
    • X: The item on the top row deals 0 damage to the item indicated on the red (last) row.

    Note:

    In addition to normal attacks, some characters have access to two types of special attacks. The first type is a melee attack, which deals half base damage to enemies. The second type is a defensive shield that multiplies the character's defense by 2 for 2 consecutive turns.
